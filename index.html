About the lab

Turning complex neural data into meaningful markers of brain function

Our research brings together neural signal processing, brain-network analysis, artificial intelligence, and clinical neuroscience. We study how brain activity is organised across space and time, how neural networks change with cognition and behaviour, and how these insights can support improved diagnosis, monitoring, and treatment of neurological disorders.

AI-NE Lab works across multiple brain-recording modalities — electroencephalography (EEG), magnetoencephalography (MEG), and stereoelectroencephalography (SEEG/iEEG) — to translate complex neural data into meaningful markers of brain function, disease, and behaviour.

Research vision

The brain is a dynamic network

Its activity changes from moment to moment, across regions, and across behavioural and clinical states. AI-NE Lab seeks to understand these dynamics using principled computational methods and intelligent analysis tools — developing reliable, interpretable, and clinically relevant approaches that connect fundamental neuroscience with real-world applications in neurology, neurotechnology, and healthcare.

Research areas

What we work on

Five connected themes spanning signals, disease, and intelligent analysis.

📡

Neural signals & brain networks

Temporal, spectral, and spatial structure of EEG, MEG, and SEEG activity, and how regions interact.

⚡

Epilepsy & clinical neurotech

Biomarkers and network dynamics from EEG/SEEG to support seizure localisation and monitoring.

🌀

MEG & neurodegeneration

Neural oscillations and connectivity in Parkinson's disease and healthy brain dynamics.

🧬

AI for neuroscience

Interpretable machine learning integrated with signal processing and domain knowledge.

💭

Emotion, cognition & behaviour

Neural mechanisms of emotion, impulsivity, cognition, and brain–behaviour relationships.
