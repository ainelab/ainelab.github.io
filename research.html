Research

From neural dynamics to brain health

We investigate how brain activity is organised across space and time, how neural networks change with cognition and behaviour, and how these insights support diagnosis, monitoring, and treatment of neurological disorders.

Research areas

Five connected themes

Spanning neural signals, clinical neurotechnology, neurodegeneration, artificial intelligence, and behaviour.

Neural signals and brain networks

We investigate the temporal, spectral, and spatial structure of neural activity recorded through EEG, MEG, and SEEG. A central focus is understanding functional and effective connectivity: how distributed brain regions interact to support cognition, emotion, behaviour, and health.

Our work examines brain-network organisation, neural oscillations, signal dynamics, and the relationship between large-scale brain activity and behaviour.

Epilepsy and clinical neurotechnology

AI-NE Lab studies epilepsy using non-invasive and invasive neural recordings, including EEG and SEEG. We are interested in identifying clinically meaningful biomarkers, characterising seizure-related network dynamics, and developing data-driven tools that may support seizure localisation, disease monitoring, and personalised clinical decision-making.

We also seek to understand how pathological neural activity emerges, spreads, and interacts with cognitive and behavioural function.

MEG and neurodegenerative disorders

We use MEG to investigate brain dynamics in healthy individuals and in neurological disorders, including Parkinson's disease. By studying neural oscillations, connectivity, and network-level alterations, we aim to identify markers that improve understanding of disease mechanisms and progression.

This work supports a broader goal of developing sensitive, interpretable neural measures of brain health and dysfunction.

Artificial intelligence for neuroscience and healthcare

We develop and apply machine-learning and artificial-intelligence methods for biomedical and neural data. Our interest is not only in predictive performance, but also in interpretable methods that can reveal meaningful physiological and clinical patterns.

AI methods are integrated with signal processing, network neuroscience, and domain knowledge to support robust analysis of complex brain data.

Emotion, cognition, and behaviour

The laboratory investigates neural mechanisms underlying emotion, impulsivity, cognition, and brain–behaviour relationships. We examine how dynamic interactions within and between brain networks relate to individual differences, cognitive states, and affective processes.

This research connects neural signals with questions that matter for mental health, decision-making, and human behaviour.

Methods & approaches

How we work

AI-NE Lab combines established and emerging methods across signal processing, network neuroscience, machine learning, and computational modelling.

  • Neural signal processing
  • EEG, MEG & SEEG analysis
  • Brain connectivity & network neuroscience
  • Machine learning & artificial intelligence
  • Statistical learning & time-series analysis
  • Dynamical systems & computational modelling
  • Spectral Graph Models
  • Graph-based Ornstein–Uhlenbeck models
  • Clinical & translational neuroscience

Mathematical and computational modelling is an important enabling approach within the lab. These methods help us develop mechanistic and interpretable accounts of neural dynamics while keeping the larger focus on brain health and neurotechnology.
